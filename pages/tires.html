TREAD DEPTH

The tread depth of your tires plays a crucial role in your car's braking ability. As the tread wears down, it takes longer to come to a stop, particularly on wet roads, increasing the risk of accidents. While the minimum legal tread depth is 2/32″ (1.6mm), it is advisable to replace tires at 4/32″ for better performance, especially on wet surfaces. If you frequently encounter snow or ice, a minimum depth of 6/32″ is recommended to ensure safe driving conditions.

Opting for higher-quality tires can significantly enhance your driving experience. Budget tires serve their purpose adequately, but premium tires offer superior grip, shorter braking distances, extended lifespan, and potential fuel savings.

The amount you choose to spend should be based on your vehicle and driving conditions. Performance car owners and those frequently driving on wet roads should invest in premium tires for optimal performance. Regular drivers in drier climates can opt for budget tires, although they will still benefit from choosing reputable brands.
